I am wildly afraid to see what happens next,
You’re never ready to leave home,
Pulling on the ginger and laying the rails,
Embrace your passing with a flower in hand, knowing it’s left all too late,
Just tell them they weren’t ready.

Pacing corridors with warm coils,
Turned over,
Filled with stammers of others and their sharp breaths,
Sure of the steps next; dead to the present.

‘It will get worse before it gets better.’,
Soon they will understand,
They’ll grow so much - kicking and screaming the whole way,
Do you think they know?
Weaving the lines across the rooms,
What were they thinking?
Being a disciple throughout,
Eating off the floor,
Whispers of regret written on infancy,
The stifled flames.

Effervescent pink,
Stapled to my wall,
The words lived-in,
The hymn as evensong;

‘Promise. Don't let yourself in
for this moment again: the end
of the afternoon, drawing the curtains,
on the glare of a garden, a whole
day of snow nobody’s trodden.’
